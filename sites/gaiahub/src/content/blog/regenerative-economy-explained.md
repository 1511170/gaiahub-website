The word "sustainability" has been co-opted. Too often it means "slightly less destructive." But the Earth does not need us to be less bad — it needs us to be **actively good**.

## From Extractive to Regenerative

Traditional economics treats nature as a subset of the economy. Regenerative economics flips this: **the economy is a subset of nature**. Every transaction must leave the system better than it found it.

## The Solar Alchemy Cycle

Gaia Market operates on a simple but profound cycle:

1. **Purchase** — Every exchange initiates energy flow
2. **Commission** — A fraction automatically serves the common good  
3. **Common Treasury** — Funds are collectively custodied for regeneration
4. **Regenerative Investment** — Capital returns to the land as vital projects

## Multi-Capital Accounting

We don't measure success only in financial returns. Gaia tracks:

- **Natural capital** — Biodiversity, soil health, water quality
- **Social capital** — Community resilience, trust networks
- **Cultural capital** — Traditional knowledge, artistic expression
- **Intellectual capital** — Open innovations, shared protocols
